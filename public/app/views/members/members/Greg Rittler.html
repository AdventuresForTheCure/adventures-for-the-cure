May 2012:

My journey to a healthier life started when I had my gallbladder removed in 2010 and the doctor told me I needed to get in shape or I was going to have serious health issues. I weighed 260lbs, had high blood pressure, and had high cholesterol. I began the long but steady process of shedding pounds and getting my body moving. Today I weigh 185lbs (still trying to get lighter so I can climb faster).

The first 12 months of getting in shape I was running & playing ultimate frisbee 7 days a week. I ran my first marathon in October 2011 (The Baltimore Marathon) 2 days after my 40th birthday.

In the fall of 2011 I began fooling around on a fixed gear bike and realized I loved riding. In January of 2012 I purchased my first road bike and started chasing some speedy riders that I met from the Bristol Road Cycling Club, an informal local group of riders near my home in Towson.

I quickly became very addicted to riding. The local group was incredibly encouraging and I learned to appreciate how great our local cycling community is. They patiently waited for me at the top of climbs as I consistently brought up the rear. I've been making steady progress ever since.

One of the most encouraging riders and my informal coach has been AFC team member Alain Philippe. Alain and I met randomly on the road in January and recognized each others names from Strava. Alain been invaluable at helping a rookie cyclist learn the ropes and improve. When I realized I wanted to join a team AFC was an easy decision knowing about all the great things AFC does and what it stands for.
