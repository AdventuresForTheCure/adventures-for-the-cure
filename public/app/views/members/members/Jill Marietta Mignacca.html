Ultramarathoner, triathlete-in-training!

  • Age: 35
  • What do you do for a living? I am the Executive Assistant to the Executive Vice-President for Academic Affairs at Baker University.
  • What kind of racing or riding do you like to do? I prefer middle and long distances, and run mainly in half-marathons. I have completed the Lincoln, NE and Richmond, VA marathons. I completed my first 50k in 2011.
  • Where is your favorite place to run? The small-town and country roads of Kansas--as well as along Kansas' numerous beautiful trails with my friends!
  • What is your favorite charity? AFC and the [Topeka, Kan.] YWCA Center for Safety and Empowerment, and Washington, DC's House of Ruth.
  • Why are you joining the AFC Women's Team? As I do more endurance events, AFC's motto, "Anyth!ng is Possible" increasingly resonates. The mission of AFC is very close to my heart: My aunt Janet died from Type I Diabetes back in the 1950s. And as an endurance athlete and personal trainer to-be, living a healthy and active lifestyle is of the utmost importance to me. I am really excited to raise awareness for diabetes and to promote the importance of living a healthy and active lifestyle while participating in endurance events!
  • Do you have a running goal, if so what is it? I hope to complete my first sprint-distance triathlon in 2013, and a 50-miler in 2014, while raising awareness for AFC and Type 1 Diabetes.
