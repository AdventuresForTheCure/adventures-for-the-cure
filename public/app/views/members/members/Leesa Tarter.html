I am married to Paul Tarter, who is already a member of AFC, and it is through him and his relationship to the team that I have learned about and become interested. I love to read and spend time with my dogs, Ginny and Gandalf. I enjoy yoga and dance. I don't bike or run very much, but I am trying to strengthen my skills in both. I am hoping to start racing competively, either this year or next year, and something that has recently made its way onto my bucket list is to start sled racing. But the real driving force for why I want to join AFC is because I love the fact that its not just about training better or harder or faster, or beating the other guy to the finish, or even making it to the podium or finish line at all, even though most of those are all good things and are things everyone should strive to better themselves in. Its about helping people, about caring more for the other guy than for yourself. Its about inspiring other people to become better, to help more, to care more, than just about themselves or their small piece of the pie.
