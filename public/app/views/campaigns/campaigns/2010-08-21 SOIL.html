Sustainable Organic Integrated Livelihoods (SOIL)

Sustainable Organic Integrated Livelihoods (SOIL) is a non-profit working in Haiti to build public composting toilets and other sustainable development projects to tackle the pressing issues of soil fertility and sanitation in Haiti. Many Haitians do not have access to toilet facilities, and sanitation-related disease symptoms such as diarrhea are a major cause of death and suffering. This has only been exacerbated with the recent earthquake. We often take our crap for granted, but dealt with properly through composting it can be a safe and productive economic resource. Composting of human wastes transforms it into a rich agricultural fertilizer that can restore soil and dramatically increase crop yields for subsistence farmers struggling with the clay-like soil. SOIL has been in Haiti for 5 years and is devoted to staying there in the long-term.
